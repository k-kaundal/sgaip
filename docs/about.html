About SGAIP

Understanding stateless identity

What is SGAIP?

SGAIP (Stateless Global Agent Identity Protocol) is a cryptographic protocol that enables global, offline-verifiable identity for humans and AI agents without requiring any form of central authority, registry, or blockchain.

At its core, SGAIP answers a fundamental question: How can we prove identity without relying on a central authority? The answer is through cryptography.

The Problem SGAIP Solves

Registry Dependency

Traditional identity systems require a registry‚Äîa government body, company database, or blockchain‚Äîto maintain records. This creates a single point of failure and requires trust in a centralized authority.

Online Verification

Most identity systems require you to be online to verify someone's identity. You need network access to query a database or blockchain.

AI Agent Identity

Current systems weren't designed for autonomous agents. There's no standard way for AI systems to establish and prove identity without going through human-centric processes.

Interoperability

Different platforms and systems use different identity mechanisms. There's no interoperable standard for proving identity across platforms.

How SGAIP Works

SGAIP works by deriving identity from cryptographic keys in a completely deterministic way:

  1. Key Generation: Generate a public-private key pair using Ed25519
  2. Identity Derivation: Compute your identity (Agent Identity Digit/AID) by hashing your public key
  3. Proof Creation: Sign any message with your private key to create a cryptographic proof
  4. Verification: Anyone can verify the proof using your public key‚Äîno registry needed

Key Insight: Your identity is not stored anywhere. It's calculated from your public key. The same calculation always produces the same identity. This means your identity is portable and verifiable anywhere without asking any authority.

Design Philosophy

  • Cryptography First: Identity based on mathematical proofs, not institutional authority
  • Minimal Primitives: Uses only Ed25519 and SHA-256‚Äîno exotic cryptography
  • Stateless: Complete information is encoded in the keys; nothing needs to be stored on a server
  • Deterministic: Same inputs always produce the same output. No randomness in identity calculation
  • Open Standard: Completely open specification with multiple independent implementations
  • Practical: Designed for real-world use, not just theoretical interest

Use Cases

For Individuals

  • Create a self-sovereign identity not tied to any company or government
  • Sign important documents with cryptographic proof of origin
  • Maintain the same identity across different platforms
  • Participate in permissionless systems while maintaining privacy

For Organizations

  • Implement permissionless identity verification systems
  • Build authentication without maintaining a user database
  • Create trustless communication channels
  • Verify the provenance of data and documents

For AI Systems

  • Autonomous agents prove their identity using cryptographic keys
  • AI systems sign actions and can be held accountable
  • Enable AI-to-AI communication with cryptographic verification
  • Create audit trails and accountability without central authorities

Limitations & What SGAIP Is Not

SGAIP solves the problem of identity derivation and cryptographic proof, but it's important to understand what it doesn't do:

  • Not a Trust System: SGAIP proves who you are, not whether you're trustworthy. You still need reputation systems, reviews, or social graphs for trust.
  • Not Authorization: Identity doesn't imply permission. You need separate authorization systems to control what people can do.
  • Not Discovery: SGAIP doesn't help you find other people or maintain contact information. It's purely identity derivation.
  • Not Revocation: There's no built-in way to "revoke" an identity. If a key is compromised, you create a new one and move forward.
  • Not Privacy: SGAIP itself doesn't hide your identity. You can use it anonymously, but additional layers are needed for privacy.

The Problem of Global Identity

Throughout human history, proving identity has required institutional authority. A passport proves identity because a government issued it. A bank account proves identity because a bank maintains records. A social media profile exists because a platform created it.

SGAIP breaks this pattern. It shows that identity can be mathematically derived, verified, and proved without any institution maintaining records. Your identity becomes a fact about your keys, not a fact about a database.
