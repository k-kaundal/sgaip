About SGAIP

Understanding stateless, quantum-resistant identity

What is SGAIP?

SGAIP (Stateless Global Agent Identity Protocol) is a cryptographic protocol that enables global, offline-verifiable identity for humans and AI agents without requiring any form of central authority, registry, or blockchain.

At its core, SGAIP answers a fundamental question: How can we prove identity without relying on a central authority? The answer is through cryptography.

The Problem SGAIP Solves

Registry Dependency

Traditional identity systems require a registry‚Äîa government body, company database, or blockchain‚Äîto maintain records. This creates a single point of failure and requires trust in a centralized authority.

Online Verification

Most identity systems require you to be online to verify someone's identity. You need network access to query a database or blockchain.

AI Agent Identity

Current systems weren't designed for autonomous agents. There's no standard way for AI systems to establish and prove identity without going through human-centric processes.

Interoperability

Different platforms and systems use different identity mechanisms. There's no interoperable standard for proving identity across platforms.

How SGAIP Works

SGAIP derives identity from cryptographic keys in a completely deterministic way:

1

Key Generation

Generate a public-private key pair using Ed25519

2

Identity Derivation

Compute your identity (Agent Identity Digit/AID) by hashing your public key

3

Proof Creation

Sign any message with your private key to create a cryptographic proof

4

Verification

Anyone can verify the proof using your public key‚Äîno registry needed

üîë Key Insight: Your identity is not stored anywhere. It's calculated from your public key. The same calculation always produces the same identity. This means your identity is portable and verifiable anywhere without asking any authority.

Limitations & What SGAIP Is Not

SGAIP solves the problem of identity derivation and cryptographic proof, but it's important to understand what it doesn't do:

‚ùå
Not a Trust System:

SGAIP proves who you are, not whether you're trustworthy. You need reputation systems for trust.

‚ùå
Not Authorization:

Identity doesn't imply permission. You need separate authorization systems.

‚ùå
Not Discovery:

SGAIP doesn't help you find people or maintain contact information. It's purely identity derivation.

‚ùå
Not Revocation:

No built-in way to "revoke" an identity. If compromised, create a new one.

‚ö†Ô∏è
Not Privacy by Default:

SGAIP doesn't hide your identity. Use it anonymously, but additional layers needed for privacy.

Research Stage:
