Design Principles

Simplicity

The protocol uses only standard cryptography (Ed25519 + SHA-256). No exotic algorithms or complex overhead.

Determinism

Identity is completely deterministic from a public key. Same input always produces the same identity.

Decentralization

No central authority or registry. Identity exists independently of any third party.

Cryptographic Proof

Identity is backed by mathematical cryptography, not social conventions or institutional authority.

Offline Verification

Proofs can be verified without internet access or connection to any service.

Permissionless

Anyone can create an identity. No registration, approval, or fees required.

Use Cases

  • AI Agent Authentication: Autonomous agents prove their identity without central registration.
  • Digital Signatures: Sign documents, messages, and transactions with offline-verifiable proofs.
  • Decentralized Systems: Build permission-less systems that require identity without central authorities.
  • Self-Sovereign Identity: Individuals maintain complete control over their identity without relying on institutions.
  • Cross-platform Identity: Same identity works across different platforms and applications.
  • Privacy-Preserving Systems: Identity proofs without revealing underlying information.
